Land Capability

Land Capability Classification is a measure of the suitability of soils for field crops. The classification system was developed by the US Department of Agriculture to inform farm planning and soil management.

Soil is divided into eight capability classes, ranging from Class 1 (soils have slight limitations that restrict agricultural use) to Class 8 (soils or area are unsuitable for agriculture). With proper management, soils in classes 1-4 can be utilized to grow crops with long-term productivity, while soils in classes 4-8 are not suitable for commercial crop production and are best used for pasture, forestland, or non-agricultural uses.

Soils are classified for both irrigated and non-irrigated agriculture; the "Land Capability" map on crop compass shows the soil classification for Oregon land suitable for growing commercial crops with irrigation. See [UDEL] for more information about each class.

Data source: [LCC]

County Rankings:

In the donut charts, the value of the measure is shown inside the circle. The filled-in proportion of the circle shows the county's value relative to the largest value from all counties.

The donut charts visualize the following measures:

Effective number of crops

A measure of crop diversity in a county that takes into account not just the number of crops grown, but also the proportion of land for each crop. If most of the farmland is a monoculture, than the effective number of crops will be one. Higher numbers indicate more diversity of crops planted on the farmed land in the selected region. See reference for defintion.

Data source: [NASS], AgCensus, year 2012

Reference: Aguilar

Growing Degree Days

Growing degree days are a measure of agricultural output based primarily on the mean daily temperature. Over the year, growing degree days accumulate for each day the mean temperature is over 50 degrees Fahrenheit. For example, if the mean temperature on a day is 53 degrees Fahrenheit, that day contributes 53 - 50 = 3 growing degree days to the annual total. Crop Compass shows the average annual growing degrees days for the selected regions over the years 1981-2010.

Data source: [Climate]

Reference: [NOAA]
